Descriptive Text

Definition of Descriptive Text

Descriptive text is a text that describes a particular object in detail. It can be its size, color, shape, smell, and so on.

Purpose of Descriptive Text

The purpose of descriptive text is to describe an object in detail. These objects can be people, animals, things, or places.

Structure of Descriptive Text

The structure of descriptive text consists of two parts, namely identification and description.

  • Identification: Introduction of the object to be described.
  • Description: Specific explanation of the object being described.

Language Features of Descriptive Texts

In writing descriptive text, there are three language features that you must use:

  • Adjective: Descriptive text is inseparable from adjectives.
  • Simple Present Tense: Commonly used to express general facts or truths.
  • Adverb of Frequency: Useful to show how often an activity is done.

Example of Descriptive Text

Identification:

Let me introduce you to my best friend. Her name is Hanni. She is my classmate in Junior High School. She is thirteen years old.

Description:

Hanni is so pretty. She has an oval face, rounded eyes, pointed nose, and beautiful black long hair. Her skin color is fair. Also, she has a slim body and is quite tall. She is 155 centimeters tall.

Hanni really loves to sing and dance. She joins the dance club as her extracurricular activity at school. She is smart and friendly. She often helps me to do my homework and always respects the elders. Everyone loves her.
